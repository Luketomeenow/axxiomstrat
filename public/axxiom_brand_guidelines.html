05 — Logo Usage

Do's & Don'ts

✓ Use on dark navy backgrounds
The full-color logo works best on Axxiom dark navy (#0B1628 or similar). This is the primary use case.
✗ Never recolor or alter the mark
Never change the logo colors, apply a single-color tint, or use the mark in any color other than the approved versions.
✓ Use on white/light backgrounds
The full-color logo also works on pure white or very light neutral backgrounds. Ensure sufficient contrast with surrounding content.
✗ Never stretch or distort
Always scale the logo proportionally from a corner handle. Never stretch, compress, or skew in any direction.
✓ Use icon mark at small sizes
When the full logo would be too small to read clearly, the icon mark alone is appropriate — app icons, profile photos, embroidery.
✗ Never place on busy/colorful backgrounds
Never place the logo on photographic backgrounds, colorful gradients, or patterns that reduce legibility.
06 — Brand Voice

How We Speak

Axxiom's voice is that of a trusted expert — confident without arrogance, direct without being cold, technically knowledgeable without being inaccessible. We speak to building professionals as peers, not clients.

Confident

We make clear statements backed by data. We don't hedge unnecessarily. When we say a problem is solved, it is.

"Your certification is current. Your next inspection is scheduled for March 14. Everything is handled."

Expert

We use precise technical language when appropriate and translate complexity into clear implications for non-technical audiences.

"Your elevator's door operator fault (DOOR-OP-CLOSE) has triggered 3 times in 90 days — we're scheduling a targeted repair before it escalates."

Proactive

We bring information to clients before they need to ask. We anticipate, flag, and act — rather than react.

"We noticed your hydraulic unit is approaching its 5-year load test window. We've already contacted the AHJ to schedule it — you'll receive confirmation this week."

Human

Behind every system is a team of people who care. We acknowledge complexity, speak plainly, and never hide behind jargon.

"That's a fair concern. Let me walk you through exactly what happened and what we're doing to make sure it doesn't happen again."

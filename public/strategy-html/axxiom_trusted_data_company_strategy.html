Pillar 01

Build the data asset — the foundation everything else rests on

You cannot claim to be a trusted data company without a data asset worth trusting. This is the internal work that happens before any public positioning move.

Step 1 — Unify and clean all historical asset data

Every elevator Axxiom has ever serviced needs a clean, complete record in Dynamics: install date, equipment type, manufacturer, all service history, all inspection outcomes, all fault codes. Right now this data likely has gaps, inconsistencies, and legacy formats. Cleaning it is unglamorous but it's what makes everything downstream credible.

→ Deliverable: unified asset master in Dataverse with data quality score per record
Step 2 — Define the safety metrics Axxiom will own

Pick 5–8 specific safety metrics that Axxiom will track, define publicly, and report on. Examples: mean time between failures by equipment type, inspection pass rate by building vertical, PM compliance rate by market, emergency call-out frequency per unit-age cohort. These become the vocabulary of Axxiom's safety authority.

→ Deliverable: Axxiom Safety Metrics Framework v1.0 — internal document, then published
Step 3 — Establish data governance and anonymisation policy

To publish data externally, Axxiom needs a clear policy on what gets published, at what aggregation level, with what client consent. Benchmark data is always anonymised at the building or fleet level — never unit-identifiable. This policy also protects Axxiom legally and builds client confidence that their data won't be exposed.

→ Deliverable: Data Governance Policy + client consent clause added to new contracts
Step 4 — Build the data flywheel

Every job completed → data point added. Every inspection → outcome logged. Every fault code → pattern updated. The system must be designed so that doing the work automatically enriches the data asset. This is what makes the data compound over time rather than requiring manual curation effort indefinitely.

→ Deliverable: automated data capture pipeline — no manual data entry for standard events

Pillar 02

Become the public voice on elevator safety

Trust is built in public. The data asset is internal. The trust positioning requires external signals that other people can point to — reports, benchmarks, safety alerts, media coverage. These are the things that make a journalist write "according to Axxiom's annual safety report" instead of "according to the company."

Annual Elevator Safety Report Flagship asset

Published every January. 10–15 page report covering: fleet-wide inspection pass rates, most common failure modes by equipment type, PM compliance rates by vertical, notable safety trends vs. prior year. Fully anonymised, data-backed, designed for PR distribution. This becomes the thing Axxiom is known for.

→ Pitched to: trade publications, facility management press, state elevator boards, insurance carriers
Safety alert bulletins

When the data shows an emerging pattern — a specific component failing at abnormal rates, a manufacturer defect trend, a seasonal issue in coastal markets — Axxiom publishes a safety alert. Sent to clients first (they feel protected), then publicly. This is how you build the "early warning system" reputation.

→ Distributed via: email to clients, LinkedIn, press release to trade media, AHJ notification
Elevator Safety Index — a public benchmark

A quarterly published score or index that rates elevator safety performance across markets, building types, or equipment cohorts. Think: a simple, repeatable metric that media can track over time. "The Axxiom Elevator Safety Index rose 4 points this quarter, driven by improved PM compliance in hospitality." This creates a recurring news hook.

→ Requires: enough data volume across markets + consistent methodology + PR support to launch
Thought leadership from the leadership team

The CEO and technical leadership need to be visible as named safety experts — not just the company brand. LinkedIn articles, industry conference panels, trade press interviews, podcast appearances on facility management shows. When a journalist calls about elevator safety, they should think of Axxiom's leadership first.

→ Target: 2 industry conference speaking slots/year + quarterly bylined article in trade press

Pillar 03

Turn data into premium client products

This is where the data positioning generates direct revenue. Clients who trust Axxiom's data want to see their own fleet benchmarked against it. These products make the trust tangible and tie clients to Axxiom's platform, not just their service contracts.

Fleet Safety Scorecard Upsell

Monthly one-page report per client showing their fleet's safety score vs. Axxiom's anonymised industry benchmark. "Your 12 units scored 87/100 — the hospitality industry average is 74." Makes the client feel ahead and want to stay ahead.

Real-time safety portal Premium tier

Client-facing dashboard in their own portal: live compliance status, risk scores, upcoming inspections, and benchmark comparison. Replaces the "call Axxiom to check status" behaviour with self-serve visibility — clients love this.

Axxiom Safety Certification Brand anchor

A formal certification program for buildings that meet Axxiom's published safety standards — full PM compliance, zero lapsed inspections, risk score above threshold. The certificate is displayed in the building lobby. Becomes a differentiator for hotel brands and healthcare systems.

Insurance-grade safety report New market

Structured safety report formatted for insurance carrier review — designed to support property owners seeking lower premiums on buildings with documented elevator safety programs. Opens a direct relationship with commercial insurers as a data partner.

The certification program is the highest-leverage play long-term. Once "Axxiom Certified" becomes a recognised signal in the market — appearing in hotel RFPs, hospital procurement criteria, and insurance assessments — Axxiom owns a standard that competitors cannot simply replicate. The data is what makes the certification credible.

Pillar 04

Build the partnerships that validate the positioning

Third-party validation is what separates a company that claims to be a data authority from one that actually is. These partnerships give Axxiom's data credibility that no amount of self-promotion can create.

Regulatory partnerships — AHJs and state elevator boards Highest credibility

Proactively share anonymised safety trend data with state elevator boards and key AHJs. Offer to be a data source for their annual safety statistics. When a regulator cites Axxiom's data in a public report, the credibility transfer is permanent. This also opens early access to regulatory changes — Axxiom becomes a partner in shaping inspection standards rather than just complying with them.

→ Start with: Florida DBPR, NYC DOB, and whichever state has the largest Axxiom market share
Insurance carrier partnerships Revenue opportunity

Commercial property insurers need verifiable elevator safety data to price risk. Axxiom can offer a structured data feed or safety report standard that carriers can accept for underwriting decisions. In exchange, Axxiom gets endorsed as the preferred safety data provider for that carrier's policyholders — which is a direct channel to every building they insure.

→ Targets: FM Global, Zurich, Liberty Mutual commercial property divisions
Industry association partnerships — NAEC and vertical associations

The National Association of Elevator Contractors (NAEC) and vertical-specific groups (American Hospital Association, AHLA for hotels) are natural distribution channels for Axxiom's safety reports. Contributing data to industry benchmarks and sponsoring safety research positions Axxiom as a contributor to the field, not just a commercial operator.

→ Path: join NAEC safety committee → contribute data → co-publish benchmark → keynote session
Real estate and facility management platform integrations

Large property managers use platforms like Yardi, MRI, and Planon for facilities management. An API integration that feeds Axxiom's safety data into these platforms turns Axxiom into an embedded data provider — the safety layer inside tools the client already uses daily. This is a long-term moat that is very hard to displace.

→ Phase 3 play — requires clean data API and partnership BD capacity

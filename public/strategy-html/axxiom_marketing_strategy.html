Current marketing audit

What Axxiom is doing now — and the honest assessment

Based on their website and news page, Axxiom's current marketing strategy can be summarised in one sentence: acquisition press releases and a static brochure website. That's it. For a $120M PE-backed company with 8 acquisitions in 2 years, this is a significant gap — and a significant opportunity.

 Gap 1 — Invisible to their buyers online

A facility director in Miami searching "elevator maintenance service Miami hospital" will not find Axxiom. No local SEO, no city pages, no content — just an acquisition press release from 2024. The OEMs and even small independents with basic SEO will outrank them for the searches that matter.

 Gap 2 — Marketing like a startup, not a $120M platform

Their entire public narrative is acquisition announcements. That's investor-facing content, not buyer-facing content. The building manager deciding which elevator company to hire doesn't care about Axxiom's PE backing — they care about whether Axxiom knows their specific vertical and won't miss an inspection.

 Gap 3 — Not using AI or data as a marketing asset yet

Their "Safety" page exists but says nothing differentiating. The AI systems being built, the compliance intelligence, the predictive data — none of this is visible externally. Competitors can't counter what Axxiom hasn't announced yet, but Axxiom also can't win deals with capabilities buyers don't know exist.

 Gap 4 — No founder / CEO brand driving awareness

Jeremy Metzger is the CEO of a fast-moving, PE-backed elevator platform executing 8 acquisitions in 2 years. That story is genuinely compelling to facility managers, building owners, and potential acquisition targets. None of it is being told publicly — his LinkedIn is not a marketing asset for the company right now.

The AI outbound system — Clay + Apollo

Find → enrich → personalise → sequence → route to CRM

Core system
Apollo: find contacts → Clay: enrich + research → AI: write personalised email → Sequence → CRM
Step 1 — Apollo finds: "Facilities Director at hotels with 100+ rooms in Miami FL" — returns 200+ contacts with title, company, LinkedIn, email
Find
Step 2 — Clay enriches each contact: property address, number of floors, building age (indicator of elevator age), current elevator vendor (if findable), recent permit activity, LinkedIn recent posts

Building age is gold — a 1990s hotel with the original elevators is a modernisation lead. Clay finds this from public property records.

Enrich
Step 3 — AI writes a personalised first line for every email: "Your Hyatt on Brickell was built in 1997 — if the elevators haven't been modernised, you're likely spending 40% more on repairs than you need to. Worth 15 minutes?"

Building-specific personalisation has 3× the reply rate of generic cold email. The AI does this for every contact automatically.

Personalise
Step 4 — 4-touch sequence: Email (personalised) → LinkedIn connection request (CEO sends) → LinkedIn voice note → Final email "closing the loop". All automated, all tracked.

Review & reputation AI — Google reviews at scale

Auto-request after every job · AI-drafted response to every review

Quick win
24 hours after job marked complete in Dynamics → AI sends personalised review request SMS/email to client contact: "Hi [Name], your Hyatt's elevator 3 service is complete. If we did a good job, a quick Google review means the world — [link]." Response rate: 15–25% vs. 2% for generic requests.
Automate
AI drafts a personalised response to every Google review within 2 hours — references the specific job, the building, the tech's name. Human approves in 30 seconds or posts as-is.

Google rewards frequent, responded-to reviews with higher local search ranking. This is free SEO.

Respond
Negative review detection: if a 1–2 star review is posted, AI flags it immediately to account manager with draft response + escalation recommendation. Never let a negative review sit unanswered for more than 2 hours.
Protect

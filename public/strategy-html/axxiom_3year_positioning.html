Competitive moats

What makes this impossible to copy by Year 3

A competitor can copy a product feature in 6 months. They cannot copy these five things without starting 3 years ago. Each moat compounds — the longer Axxiom holds the position, the harder it gets for anyone else to close the gap.

01

The proprietary safety dataset

3 years of structured, validated, fault-coded service data across thousands of units in multiple markets and verticals. A new entrant can build the system in 6 months — they cannot build 3 years of clean historical data. The dataset IS the moat.

Uncopiable
02

Regulatory relationships and citation

Being named in a state elevator board's annual report as a data source takes years of relationship building. Once Axxiom is cited, no competitor can cite the same AHJ. The first mover owns the relationship.

First mover
03

The Axxiom Safety Certification standard

A certification that appears in procurement documents is a lock-in mechanism. Buildings won't switch elevator companies if switching means losing the certification. And competitors can't offer the same certification — it's Axxiom's IP.

Lock-in
04

AHJ rules database

Every AHJ jurisdiction mapped, maintained, and embedded in the dispatch compliance gate. Axxiom can enter a new market in days. A competitor starting from zero needs 6–12 months to build the same coverage. This accelerates market expansion at a rate no competitor can match.

Speed moat
05

Platform network effects

Once SaaS operators and facility management platforms integrate with Axxiom's data API, every new unit added to the network improves the dataset for everyone. This is a classic network effect — more data makes the product better, which attracts more users, which generates more data.

Network effect

Honest assessment

The risks that could derail this — and how to hedge them

Every strategic plan looks clean on paper. These are the three scenarios most likely to slow or stop Axxiom's 3-year arc — and what to do about each one.

 Risk 1 — An elevator OEM (Otis, KONE, Schindler) launches their own AI platform first

The OEMs have the scale, the install base, and the capital. If Otis launches a safety data platform in 2025, Axxiom's platform play is much harder. The hedge: move fast on the regulatory and insurance partnerships in Year 1–2. OEMs are slow to work with regulators and will avoid insurance partnerships (conflicts with their OEM liability interests). Axxiom can own those relationships before any OEM gets there.

 Risk 2 — Internal adoption failure — techs and dispatchers don't use the system

The most common reason AI systems fail in field service is not technology — it's adoption. If techs find workarounds to avoid the structured job close form, the data pipeline breaks and the entire strategy collapses. The hedge: involve techs in the design from Week 1, make the form faster than free text, and tie performance reviews to data quality metrics (not just job completion). Make compliance the path of least resistance.

 Risk 3 — Scaling operations faster than the data quality can keep up

If Axxiom enters 5 new markets in Year 2 and the data foundation isn't solid, the safety report becomes unreliable and the regulatory partnerships become embarrassing rather than credibility-building. The hedge: market expansion in Year 1–2 is limited to markets where the AHJ database is already complete and the data pipeline is already running. Never enter a market where you can't immediately capture clean data from day one.

The one thing that guarantees failure

Treating the AI as a cost-reduction tool rather than a competitive positioning tool.
If Axxiom's leadership sees "dispatch automation" as a way to reduce dispatcher headcount and nothing more, they will capture 10% of the value and miss the 90% that comes from using the same system to build a data asset, earn regulatory relationships, and own an industry standard. The technology is the same. The ambition is the difference.

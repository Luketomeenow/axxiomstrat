Week 1 execution plan for Axxiom Elevator engagement — day by day tasks and deliverables

Week 1 war plan

Five days. One goal: earn the right to build.

Week 1 is not about building anything. It's about showing up with the right questions, getting the right access, and ending the week with a signed scope and a discovery report that proves you understand their business better than their last vendor did.

Day-by-day focus

Mon  Discovery meeting — understand the business and the pain AM + PM
Tue  Systems audit — get inside Dynamics and map what exists Full day
Wed  Field interviews — talk to dispatchers and 2 field techs Full day
Thu  Synthesis — build the gap analysis and priority matrix Full day
Fri  Readout — present findings, agree scope, get sign-off AM readout

What you must walk away with by Friday 5pm

Dynamics 365 read access confirmed By Mon EOD
Data completeness score on asset records (sample of 100) By Wed EOD
List of all data sources outside Dynamics (spreadsheets, paper, portals) By Wed EOD
Top 3 operational pain points ranked by Axxiom leadership By Mon EOD
Signed Phase 1 scope document By Fri EOD
Week 1 discovery report sent to client Fri EOD

Monday

Discovery — understand the business and the pain

Two meetings. One with leadership to get strategic context. One with the ops team to understand day-to-day friction. Come in with prepared questions, not a sales pitch. Your job today is to listen and take precise notes.

AM

Leadership discovery session (90 mins)

CEO / COO / VP Operations · in person or Zoom

Do not skip
"Walk me through a day in the life of your dispatch team right now — from when a call comes in to when the tech is on site."

Listen for: where it breaks down, what they do manually, where they lose time

Q1
"When a client calls angry about an elevator, what's the most common reason? What do you have to do to fix it?"

This reveals the ops gaps that cost the most in client relationships

Q2
"How do you currently track which technicians are certified for which jurisdictions? Where does that live?"

The answer to this will tell you exactly how much compliance risk they're carrying

Q3
"You're expanding into new markets. What's the biggest operational headache when you go into a new city?"

This surfaces the scaling pain — the thing AI can directly solve

Q4
"If you could fix one thing in operations in the next 90 days that would have the biggest impact — what is it?"

This is your Phase 1 priority. Whatever they say becomes the first deliverable.

Most important
Note: Record the session (with permission). You will reference these exact words in the Friday readout.
PM

Ops team deep dive (2 hrs)

Dispatch manager + 1 account manager · informal, their workspace

Operational gold
Watch them process a live service request from start to dispatch. Don't interrupt — just observe. Count the number of screens they switch between and the number of manual decisions they make.

Target number to note: how many minutes from intake call to tech assigned?

Observe first
"What's the thing you do every day that you wish you never had to do again?" — ask the dispatcher, not management
Key question
"When a tech goes to the wrong job or doesn't have the right cert, how does that get caught?" — listen for where the compliance process actually lives vs. where leadership thinks it lives
Key question
Ask to see any spreadsheets or tools they use outside of Dynamics — "Is there anything you track in Excel or somewhere else that isn't in the system?"

Write down every external tool or file they mention. This is the shadow data inventory starting.

Data audit start
End of day: You should have 2–3 pages of notes and a clear answer to "what is the single most painful thing in their ops right now."
EOD

Monday wrap — access requests and prep for Tuesday

Solo · 30 mins

Admin
Send a follow-up email to your Axxiom contact confirming: Dynamics 365 read-only access needed by Tuesday 8am, contact names for Wednesday field tech interviews, any reporting exports or spreadsheets the ops team mentioned
Send tonight
Spend 20 mins organising your notes into 3 buckets: Things that confirm the plan, Things that surprised you, Questions that need answering Tuesday/Wednesday
Prep
Output: Access request sent · Shadow data list started · Top 3 pain points identified and ranked

Tuesday

Systems audit — get inside Dynamics

Full day in the system. You're not building anything — you're measuring. Run the completeness scripts, map the data structure, and document every gap. This is the most technical day of the week.

1

Map the Dynamics 365 Field Service setup

Solo · 2–3 hrs · read-only access

First thing
Check what modules are active: Field Service, Customer Service, Sales? Which ones are actually being used vs. licensed but idle?
Map
Look at the work order structure: what fields exist, which are mandatory vs. optional, what's consistently filled in vs. mostly blank
Map
Look at technician profiles: do they store certification details? Licence expiry dates? AHJ registrations? Or is it just name and phone number?

This is the compliance gate readiness check. If certs aren't in Dynamics, that's a P1 gap.

Critical check
Check asset records: how many exist, what fields are populated, is there a field for AHJ jurisdiction, install date, equipment type?
Critical check
Check what Power Automate flows already exist — sometimes there are half-built automations from previous attempts. Don't rebuild what's already there.
Quick win check
Output: Dynamics capability map — a simple table: feature, current state, gap, priority
2

Run completeness score on 100 asset records

Xenoz · export to Excel, score manually or with script · 2 hrs

Key data point
Export a random sample of 100 asset records to Excel. Pick across markets and building types — not just one region.
Sample
Score each record against 10 critical fields: install date, equipment type, manufacturer, serial number, AHJ jurisdiction, last PM date, last inspection date, inspection outcome, technician cert data linked, client site address complete

1 point per field present and populated = max 10. Calculate average across 100 records.

Key metric
Identify the 3 fields with the highest absence rate. These become the cleaning priorities in Step 3.
Finding
Output: Average completeness score (e.g. "6.2/10") + top 3 missing fields. This number goes on slide 1 of Friday's readout.
3

Check the tech notes field — assess fault code chaos

Solo · 1 hr · export last 200 work orders

Revealing
Export the notes/description field from the last 200 completed work orders. Paste into a word cloud tool or just scan manually.

What you're looking for: are there patterns ("door", "stuck", "motor") or is it truly random free text?

Quick analysis
Check: does Axxiom have any existing fault code or job type taxonomy? Even informal categories? If yes, this simplifies the cleaning step significantly.
Check
Output: Single finding: "Tech notes are [structured/semi-structured/completely free text]" — this determines how much AI extraction work is needed in Step 3.

Wednesday

Field interviews — the truth lives in the field

The system tells you what was recorded. The techs tell you what actually happens. These conversations will surface the shadow data, the workarounds, and the real compliance gaps that no dashboard shows.

1

Interview 2 field technicians (45 mins each)

Pick 1 senior tech + 1 tech with under 2 years at Axxiom · informal

Do not skip
"When you finish a job, what do you actually write in the system — and what do you leave out because it takes too long?"

This is the data loss question. The answer tells you what the pipeline is currently missing.

Key question
"Has there ever been a job where you showed up and something was wrong — wrong parts, wrong site, wrong elevator — that could have been avoided if you'd been told something different before you left?"

This surfaces the dispatch quality gap and the case for AI job briefs

Key question
"When you service a unit you've worked on before, do you remember details about it — or do you have to figure it out again on site?"

This reveals whether the asset familiarity score would actually be used and valued

Question
"What's the one thing that would make your job easier that nobody has fixed yet?"

Techs give the most honest answers to this. What they say often becomes the best UX feature in the job close form.

Gold
Output: Direct quotes from 2 techs. Use their exact words in the Friday readout — clients respond to hearing their team's actual voice reflected back.
2

Interview the compliance / office coordinator (45 mins)

The person who handles inspection scheduling and certificate renewals

High value
"How do you currently track inspection due dates and certificate renewals? Show me."

The answer is almost always a spreadsheet. Get a copy. That spreadsheet is P1 data that needs to be in Dynamics.

Show me
"Has Axxiom ever had an elevator with a lapsed certificate operate — even briefly — because it wasn't caught in time?"

This is a sensitive question. Ask it gently. The answer justifies the entire compliance tracker system.

Important
"How much time per week do you spend on compliance-related admin — chasing inspectors, filing certificates, tracking renewals?"

Get a number. Even a rough estimate. This becomes the ROI calculation in the scope document.

ROI data
Output: Compliance spreadsheet copy · hours/week estimate on compliance admin · any recent compliance near-misses documented
3

Complete the shadow data inventory

Based on all interviews · 1 hr · compile and close gaps

Compile
List every data source mentioned across all interviews: Dynamics, compliance spreadsheet, any regional Excel files, paper binders, email certificate attachments, third-party inspection portals, tech personal notes
Document all
For each source: what data, how many records, date range, who owns it, format (digital/paper), can it be migrated to Dynamics?
Detail
Output: Complete shadow data source list — this feeds directly into the data audit report and cleaning plan

Thursday

Synthesis — build the gap analysis

Desk day. No meetings. Take everything from Monday–Wednesday and turn it into the two documents that matter: the gap analysis and the Phase 1 scope. This is the work that makes Friday's readout land.

1

Write the gap analysis — 1 page, sharp

Solo · 2 hrs · this becomes the core of Friday's readout

Core deliverable
Structure: 3 columns — Gap, Business impact (use their words from interviews), Recommended fix. Max 8–10 rows. One page.

Gaps should map directly to the systems in the pitch deck: dispatch, compliance, predictive maintenance, data foundation

Format
Highlight the top 3 gaps that have the highest impact AND are fastest to fix. These are the Phase 1 scope.

The rule: Phase 1 must show visible results in 10 weeks. Do not scope anything that takes longer than that to show an outcome.

Key decision
Output: 1-page gap analysis table — clean, no jargon, uses Axxiom's own language from the interviews
2

Draft the Phase 1 scope document

Solo · 2 hrs · this gets signed Friday

Gets signed Friday
Scope document structure: Problem statement (1 paragraph using their words) · Deliverables (3–5 specific, measurable outputs) · Timeline (10 weeks, phased) · What Axxiom provides (Dynamics access, key contacts, interview time) · Investment
Structure
Phase 1 recommended scope based on this week: Dispatch agent (intake → compliance gate → dispatch) + Data foundation (audit + schema + cleaning sprint). These two are the highest impact and the most visible.

Do not scope everything in Week 1. Scope what you can deliver excellently in 10 weeks. Expand later.

Discipline
Include a ROI line: "Based on interviews, your team spends X hrs/week on manual dispatch and Y hrs/week on compliance admin. Phase 1 is designed to reduce both by 60%+ within 10 weeks."
ROI anchor
Output: Phase 1 scope document — 1–2 pages, ready to review Friday morning and sign Friday afternoon
3

Build the Friday readout — 10 slides max

Solo · 2 hrs · use the pitch deck as a template

Presentation
Slide structure: What we learned (3 key findings from interviews) → What the data shows (completeness score, top gaps) → What we recommend (Phase 1 scope) → What happens next (timeline + sign-off)
Structure
Use direct quotes from the interviews — put them on slides with the person's role (not name if sensitive). "One of your senior techs told us: '...'" is more powerful than any data point.
Powerful move
Output: Readout deck ready to present · scope document attached as an appendix

Friday

Readout — present findings and get sign-off

This is the meeting that converts the engagement. You're not selling — you're presenting back what you learned and showing that you understand their business well enough to fix it. The scope document should feel like a natural conclusion, not a pitch.

AM

Week 1 readout presentation (60 mins)

Leadership + ops manager · same group as Monday AM

Critical meeting
Open with: "Here's what we found this week." NOT "Here's what we want to build." Frame everything as findings, not proposals.
Framing
Lead with the completeness score: "Your asset records average X/10 completeness. The 3 fields most commonly missing are [X], [Y], [Z]. This is why [specific problem they mentioned Monday] keeps happening."

Connect the data gap directly to the pain they described. This is the moment they go from "interesting" to "we need to fix this."

Key moment
Play back the tech and ops team quotes. "Your dispatcher told us [exact quote]. Your senior tech told us [exact quote]." Leadership often doesn't know what their team experiences daily.
Resonates
Present the Phase 1 scope as the logical conclusion: "Based on what we found, here's the specific work we recommend for the next 10 weeks, with these deliverables and this investment." Walk through it line by line.
Close
The close line: "We'd like to start Week 1 of the build on Monday. Are there any questions before we confirm the scope?"
PM

Scope sign-off and Week 2 kick-off planning

Solo / with Axxiom contact · 1 hr

Objective
Get the scope document signed or verbal confirmation with email confirmation if a full signature isn't possible Friday. Do not leave without clarity on whether you're proceeding.
Must-have
Book the Week 2 kick-off call: confirm Dynamics full access, schedule the compliance officer for the governance session, confirm which market starts the data cleaning pilot
Next step
Send the Week 1 discovery report by Friday 6pm: 2-page document covering key findings, completeness score, shadow data inventory, and Phase 1 scope summary. This becomes the reference document for the entire engagement.
Send today
End of Week 1: Scope signed · discovery report sent · Week 2 kick-off booked · Axxiom trusts that you know their business. Build starts Monday.

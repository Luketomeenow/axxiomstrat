Step 2 of 7 · Content System
Build Content AI Actually Wants to Cite

AI engines don't cite the most popular page — they cite the most extractable, accurate, and well-structured answer. Axxiom needs a content architecture specifically engineered for AI retrieval, not just human readers.

A
Rule #1 — The Most Important Change
Answer First. Always.
Every page, every blog post, every FAQ must lead with a direct 40–60 word answer to the question it targets — before any context, intro, or explanation.
›
⚠️ The #1 reason Axxiom's content won't get cited: burying the answer. AI systems extract the first clear, complete answer to the query. If your first paragraph is "Great question, elevator maintenance is important for many reasons…" — the AI skips you.
The 40–60 Word Rule: Open every H2 section with a paragraph that directly answers the section heading as a question. No warm-up. No transitional phrases. State the answer, then support it. This is the single highest-leverage content change for AEO.
❌ WRONG — How Axxiom might be writing content now
"Elevator maintenance is a critical part of managing any commercial property. Building owners and property managers often wonder about the best approach to keeping their elevators running. There are many factors to consider, including the age of the equipment, the building type..."
✅ RIGHT — Answer-first format for AI citation
"Commercial elevators should be serviced by a certified technician every 1–3 months, depending on usage volume and equipment age. Buildings with more than 10 floors or high daily traffic (over 200 trips/day) require monthly preventive maintenance to stay code-compliant and avoid costly breakdowns. Most states require annual inspections by a licensed elevator inspector."

C
Rule #3 — Content Formatting
Format Everything for Machine Extraction
How content is formatted matters as much as what it says. These structural rules make Axxiom's content far more likely to be extracted and cited by AI systems.
›
Heading Structure
  • Every H2 must be phrased as a question: "How often should elevators be inspected?" not "Inspection Frequency"
  • H3s should contain the direct short answer: "Every 1–3 months depending on usage"
  • Never put important answers inside long paragraphs buried in the middle of content
Definition Blocks
  • For any industry term (elevator modernization, full maintenance contract, AHJ, ASME A17.1), create a formatted definition block: [Term]: one clear sentence definition + supporting paragraph
  • Wrap in DefinedTerm schema (covered in Schema tab)
  • These answer standalone AI queries like "what is elevator modernization"
Tables & Lists
  • Use comparison tables wherever a "what's the difference between X and Y" question exists
  • Use numbered lists for processes: "How to prepare for an elevator inspection: 1. 2. 3…"
  • Tables extract cleanly into AI-generated answers and are preferred citation format
Content Freshness
  • Update key pages every 90 days — add the date at the top: "Last updated: [Month Year]"
  • AI systems favor recently updated content for compliance/regulatory queries
  • Set a quarterly content refresh calendar across all 7 brand sites
Step 3 of 7 · Technical Foundation
Schema Markup — The Language AI Uses to Cite You

Schema markup is structured data code added to each webpage that tells AI systems exactly what your content means. Pages with proper schema are 40% more likely to be cited by AI engines. FAQPage schema alone gives a 41% AI citation rate vs. 15% without it. This is non-negotiable.

Priority for Axxiom: Implement these 5 schema types across all 8 websites (Axxiom corporate + 7 brands). JSON-LD format, validated through Google's Rich Results Test before publishing. This is a one-time build with ongoing maintenance — the ROI is compounding.
1
Schema Type 1 · Homepage of all 8 sites

Tells AI systems who Axxiom is, what it does, where it operates, and why it should be trusted. This is the foundational entity signal that every other schema builds on.

Step 5 of 7 · Platform Targeting
Platform-by-Platform Optimization

Each AI platform has slightly different citation behavior. The foundation (content + schema + authority) works across all of them, but there are platform-specific tactics that improve citation rates on each.

Google AI Overviews (Highest Priority)

Appears in 55% of Google searches. Most likely where property managers encounter Axxiom first. Feeds from Google's index — traditional SEO foundation matters most here.

  • Rank first on Google — AI Overviews heavily (not exclusively) pull from top-ranking pages. SEO and AEO must work together here
  • Featured Snippet optimization — use the exact question as an H2, follow immediately with a 40–55 word direct answer paragraph
  • People Also Ask domination — target every PAA question in your FAQ pages. These feed directly into AI Overviews
  • Google Business Profile — fully optimized GBP is cited directly in local AI Overviews for service queries
  • Core Web Vitals — page load speed and mobile performance affect AI Overview eligibility
ChatGPT Search (Rising Fast)

883M monthly users. ChatGPT now browses the web and cites sources. Its citation behavior differs from Google — it favors comprehensive, well-organized content with clear entity signals.

  • Bing indexing — ChatGPT's web search uses Bing. Submit all 8 sitemaps to Bing Webmaster Tools immediately
  • Comprehensive pages win — ChatGPT tends to cite longer, more thorough pages over short ones. Target 1,500–3,000 words on pillar pages
  • Brand mentions matter — get "Axxiom Elevator" mentioned by name on third-party sites (directories, press, partners)
  • Clear entity structure — Organization schema + consistent NAP across all directories helps ChatGPT identify Axxiom as a trustworthy entity
Perplexity AI (Research-Stage Buyers)

Heavy usage among researchers and B2B buyers doing vendor due diligence. Cites sources visibly and aggressively. A Perplexity citation is a direct link the buyer can click.

  • Data-rich pages get cited most — include statistics, benchmarks, and sourced facts. Perplexity trusts pages that cite their own sources
  • Authority domain links — get linked from .gov, .edu, or industry association (.naec.org) pages — Perplexity weights these domains heavily
  • Fresh content signal — Perplexity favors recently updated content. Add "Last Updated" dates and refresh quarterly
  • Target "best elevator company in [city]" queries — Perplexity is commonly used for vendor comparison research
Google Gemini + AI Mode

Google's AI Mode (now separate from AI Overviews) provides deeper, more conversational answers. It's used by more research-intensive searchers — ideal for catching building owners in evaluation mode.

  • Topical authority clusters — Gemini favors sites that are comprehensive on a topic, not just good on individual pages. Build interconnected content across all elevator topics
  • Structured data is amplified — Gemini's retrieval is particularly schema-sensitive. All schema from Step 3 applies doubly here
  • YouTube content — Gemini (being Google) incorporates YouTube video content. Short educational videos on Axxiom's YouTube channel feed Gemini citations
Voice Search — The Silent AEO Channel

Voice assistants (Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant) pull from the same AI Overview and featured snippet sources. For elevator service, voice searches happen when someone is physically standing in a building lobby, phone in hand, frustrated. Target queries like: "Hey Google, who repairs elevators near me" and "What do I do if my elevator breaks" — these need direct, 1–2 sentence answers at the top of relevant pages.

Step 7 of 7 · Track What Matters
How to Measure AEO Success for Axxiom

AEO success isn't measured the same way as SEO. The metrics that matter are citation share, AI referral traffic, and ultimately — qualified consultation requests attributable to AI-sourced discovery.

The 4 AEO Metrics Axxiom Must Track
Metric 1 — AI Citation Share

What it is: % of target queries (from Step 1) where Axxiom or its brands are cited in AI Overviews, ChatGPT, or Perplexity responses.

How to track: Manual testing — run each target query in each AI platform and record whether Axxiom is cited. Use a spreadsheet. Do this monthly for all 50+ priority queries.

Tools: Peec.ai, Brandwatch AI Monitor, or Semrush AI Overview tracking

Metric 2 — AI-Referred Traffic

What it is: Visitors arriving from ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews. These visitors convert at 4.4× the rate of organic search visitors.

How to track: In Google Analytics 4 — create a segment for referrals from chat.openai.com, perplexity.ai, and claude.ai. Also monitor for "direct" traffic spikes that correlate with AI coverage.

Target: Track month-over-month growth. Even 20 AI-referred visitors/month who convert at 4.4× is worth more than 88 organic visitors.

Metric 3 — Featured Snippet Capture Rate

What it is: % of target queries where Axxiom owns the featured snippet (Position 0) — the box at the top of Google. Featured snippets are the primary feed for Google AI Overviews.

How to track: Google Search Console — filter by query, look for featured snippet annotations. Tools: SEMrush, Ahrefs.

Target: Win featured snippets on at least 10 priority queries within 90 days of content launch.

Metric 4 — Consultation Requests from AI Traffic

What it is: The bottom line — how many free consultation requests or calls originated from AI-sourced visitors. This ties AEO directly to revenue.

How to track: UTM parameters on all internal CTAs, call tracking numbers on each brand site, GA4 goal conversions filtered by AI referral source.

Target: Attribute at least 5–10 consultation requests/month per brand to AI traffic by Month 3.

Campaign Strategy

Maintenance contracts are Axxiom's highest-LTV offer — a signed contract means 12–36 months of guaranteed recurring revenue. The campaign goal is not awareness. It is getting a property manager to replace their current elevator vendor with Axxiom. Every element of this campaign must make switching feel safe, easy, and obviously right.

Core Insight
Property managers don't hate their elevator company — they just hate being ignored. The #1 reason buildings switch maintenance vendors is not price. It's communication failures: missed calls, late technicians, no service reports, vague invoices. Axxiom's campaign must weaponize these frustrations — not by attacking competitors, but by making Axxiom's responsiveness and transparency feel like a relief.

Contract Types to Lead With
Full Maintenance
Lead with this. Most repairs included. Highest monthly value. Best LTV for Axxiom. Position it as the "we handle everything" package — zero surprises on repair bills.
Partial Maintenance
Present as the middle option. Maintenance covered + selected repairs. Good upsell path. Use for properties with newer equipment that don't need full coverage yet.
Oil & Grease
Entry-level. Never lead with this — it's the door opener for "non-contract" buildings. Goal: sign O&G first, then upgrade after first repair event.

Campaign Strategy

Modernization is a capital expenditure decision — building owners and asset managers make it, not day-to-day property managers. The sales cycle is 60–120 days. The campaign must educate, build trust, and make the ROI case long before anyone asks for a price. Position modernization as a proactive investment with measurable returns — not a reactive fix.

Core Insight: Building owners delay modernization because they don't know when the right time is. They think of it as a cost, not an investment. Axxiom's campaign must reframe modernization as: (1) protection against escalating repair costs, (2) a property value multiplier, and (3) a tenant retention strategy. The numbers do the selling — Axxiom's job is to get the numbers in front of the right people.
Modernization Trigger Events (When to Target)
Equipment Age
Elevators 20+ years old = prime modernization window. Controller/drive systems from early 2000s are now obsolete — parts availability is declining fast. Target buildings with known older equipment.
Rising Repair Costs
If a building has had 3+ major repair events in 12 months, modernization ROI is almost always positive within 3–5 years. Use this math in all content.
Building Sale / Refinancing
Modernized elevators increase asset valuation. Target property transactions — LOOPNET listings, CBRE/JLL newsletters, commercial real estate groups on LinkedIn.
Tenant Complaints
Ride quality, slow doors, outdated interiors = lease renewal risk. Position cab and door modernization as a tenant experience investment — directly tied to NOI.
Code Requirements
New code mandates (DLM, SPB upgrades) often open a conversation about full modernization. Compliance work is the door-opener to a larger project conversation.
Budget Planning Season
Most capital budget decisions made in Q4 for the following year. Run highest-spend modernization campaigns Sept–Nov to be in the room during budget allocation.

Campaign Strategy

Code compliance leads are driven by anxiety — the fear of violations, fines, lawsuits, and reputational damage. Axxiom's role in this campaign is not fear-mongering but expert guidance. Be the calm, authoritative voice that helps building owners navigate a confusing regulatory landscape before an inspector does it for them — less favorably.

Core Insight: Most building owners don't know their compliance status until an inspector tells them — and by then, it's a violation, not a question. Axxiom can own this space by being the brand that educates first, positions compliance work as protection (not just a checkbox), and — critically — uses compliance conversations as the entry point for DLM upgrades, SPB upgrades, Cat 1 & 5 testing, and ultimately, maintenance contracts.
Compliance Trigger Events — Target These Moments
Annual Inspection Window
Most states require annual elevator inspections. Target buildings 30–60 days before their typical inspection window. "Is your elevator inspection-ready?" campaigns run in the 2 months prior to peak inspection seasons in each state.
Category 5 Deadline
5-year full-load safety test required by most jurisdictions. Buildings that haven't done one in 4+ years need urgent outreach. This test requires advance scheduling and often surfaces components needing repair before the test can pass.
DLM Enforcement Expansion
Door Lock Monitoring requirements are expanding state by state. Arizona is ahead — other markets are catching up. Target older buildings in DLM-enforcement states before violations are issued. "Do you know if your elevator needs DLM compliance work?"
SPB Upgrade Wave
Single Plunger Brake enforcement is creating a large pool of non-compliant hydraulic elevators. Target apartments, condos, churches, schools, medical offices — all common hydraulic elevator users who may not know they have an SPB issue.
Violation Notice Received
Target via LinkedIn + Google the audience of property managers who searched for "elevator violation" or "elevator code citation." These are the hottest possible leads — they have a problem and need it solved immediately.
Building Acquisition Due Diligence
Investors acquiring buildings want elevator compliance assessments before closing. Position Axxiom's consulting/inspection service as the pre-acquisition due diligence provider. Target: commercial real estate attorneys, CBRE/JLL brokers, property investors.
